About the Project

This project analyzes job offers in the data field to uncover typical patterns across companies, skills, and regions. It combines descriptive statistics, text mining, and clustering methods to reveal how jobs naturally group together.

üé• Watch the Explanation Video

Objective

The objective of this project is to make the Data job market readable, transparent, and genuinely explorable. Job offers are numerous but highly fragmented, written with inconsistent terminology, and evolving extremely fast.

The core idea is to build a living observatory that captures, structures, and visualizes market trends in real time ‚Äî turning thousands of job descriptions into an intelligible map.

Motivation

The main motivation came from a simple challenge: understanding what the Data job market truly expects. Platforms like LinkedIn display offers individually, but they fail to provide:

Global Trends

Understanding how roles evolve month after month.

Skill Evolution

Tracking which tools are rising or declining.

Hidden Regularities

Patterns that are invisible at the scale of individual offers.

Temporal Comparison

A timeline of how the job market behaves over time.

This platform is not a job search engine, it is a semantic and analytical observatory of the Data job market.

Main Tasks

  • Designing and stabilizing the monthly Python scraper,
  • Normalizing job titles and metadata,
  • Automatically extracting technical skills, tools, soft skills, hybrid policy, visa, etc.,
  • Analyzing temporal trends and regional patterns,
  • Building interactive D3.js visualizations,
  • Developing the website structure and navigation,
  • Automating the full monthly pipeline from scraping to visualization.

Expected Results

Skill Trends

Identify emerging or declining technical skills.

Regional Insights

Structural Analysis

Reveal how the market is organized internally.

Most of these goals are already achieved and will strengthen as the database grows.

Problems Encountered

Several major challenges shaped the direction of the project:

  • Early clustering attempts produced unusable results (one giant cluster biased by company names). To overcome this, I adopted BERTopic, which combines Transformer embeddings, HDBSCAN for density-based clustering, and c-TF-IDF for naming topics. The clusters were then projected using UMAP and visualized in D3.js. However, I am still not fully satisfied with the embeddings and aim to improve them.
  • The dataset contains a temporal gap between June and October. I decided to treat October as the true starting point of the monthly timeline, and will address the gap in future iterations.

These issues pushed the project toward a more analytical platform and less toward raw clustering reliance.

Key Insights

Semantic Topic Modeling

Using BERTopic, job offers are grouped into meaningful themes based on Transformer embeddings, HDBSCAN clustering, and c-TF-IDF labeling. This reveals coherent job families such as Data Engineering, BI, Machine Learning, or Analytics.

Evolving Skills

The analysis highlights both dominant and emerging technical skills across the market. Combined with monthly scraping, it allows tracking how toolsets and expectations shift over time.

Regional Patterns

By aggregating job metadata (country, hybrid policy, seniority), the observatory uncovers geographic concentrations, seasonal peaks, and regional differences across the Data job market.

Interactive Map

A fully custom D3.js interface enables users to explore thousands of offers through an interactive map: zoom, filtering, cluster focus, and detailed job panels provide a new way to read the job ecosystem.

Automated Pipeline

The platform integrates a recurring scraper, automated column generation (skills, tools, soft skills, hybrid policy, visa), and a reproducible analysis pipeline ‚Äî turning the project into a living, updating system.
